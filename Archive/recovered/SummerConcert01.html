Stanford Summer Chorus & Orchestra presents...

Copland, Fanfare for the Common Man

Written in 1942 by American composer Aaron Copland, this patriotic fanfare for brass and percussion is now a beloved classic. The piece pays tribute to those who fought in World War II, but it has also become closely associated with the egalitarian ideals of the Olympic Games.


Jennings, Where the Sun Will Never Go Down

Joe Jennings, director of the famed SF-based men's chorus, Chanticleer, wrote this a cappella arrangement featuring several American spiritual melodies. From "Ain't-a That Good News" to "Swing Low, Sweet Chariot", the piece covers the spectrum from jubilation to sorrow, and back again.


Kodály, Budavári Te Deum

Hungarian composer Zoltan Kodály is widely recognized as one of the greatest leaders of the early 20th-century cultural revival in Hungary. His music is deeply influenced by the folk music of his homeland, which gives it a unique, exotic quality. Kodály's Te Deum was written in 1936 to celebrate the 250th anniversary of the reconquest of Buda from the Turks. The piece is wonderfully joyous and brilliant. It features four vocal soloists in addition to the 135-voice chorus and the orchestra.


Vaughan Williams, Serenade to Music

This piece features the orchestra with sixteen vocal soloists. The Serenade to Music was premiered at the Royal Albert Hall in London on October 5, 1938. The performance featured some of the best-known singers of the time, all of whom were familiar to the composer. Our performance will feature sixteen talented soloists from around the Bay Area.
