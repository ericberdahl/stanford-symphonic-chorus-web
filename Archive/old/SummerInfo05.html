Rafael Ornes will direct the Summer Chorus this year.

Repertoire

The main work will be The Armed Man: A Mass for Peace by Karl Jenkins. The Armed Man is a large-scale work which was written in 2000, and has become hugely popular in the UK. This will be the first complete performance in the United States. Here are some links about the composer and the work:

  • Amazon UK website, with info, samples, review and CD.
  • recent article about Karl Jenkins from The Herald.
  • June 10, 2005 Bay Area review of the CD - The Armed Man.
  • Karl Jenkins' Homepage.

There will also be a small set of spirituals on the theme of war and peace. Here is a list of the spirituals:

  • Battle of Jericho, arr. Moses Hogan
  • Deep River, arr. Robert Shaw and Alice Parker
  • Steal Away, arr. Joseph Jennings
  • Hope for Resolution, arr. Caldwell/Ivory

The spirituals will be performed a cappella, and The Armed Man will be performed with a chamber ensemble.

To Join

It's not hard.... just come to the first rehearsal! Membership is open to everyone; singers typically come from all corners of the Bay Area, and from all age groups and ability levels. It's not a bad idea to come a little early to the first rehearsal to allow time to sign up.

Fees

Participation is free for students; others will be charged $30. Music will be available for purchase at the first two rehearsals.
NOTE: The score for Jenkins' The Armed Man is now available in the Stanford Bookstore (in the textbook area under 'Music'). it is $25.00. All other scores will be provided for free.
