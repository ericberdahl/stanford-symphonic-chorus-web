Joining the Chorus

The Stanford Chorus is open to all students, faculty, staff and any other residents of the community interested in singing wonderful music, just like these folks. New members will be auditioned prior to or soon after the first rehearsal of each quarter. To schedule an audition before the first rehearsal (preferable for Fall Quarter at least) contact our director, Steve Sano, at sano@leland.stanford.edu If this isn't possible, simply come to the first rehearsal. The purpose of the audition is not to weed people out; rather it gives our director a chance to get to know the voices which will comprise the Chorus.

Members who are not students or faculty pay a modest (currently $30) participation fee. This money helps to offset expenses, the largest of which is hiring orchestras.

Music

Obtaining Music

The music to be performed can be purchased at the Stanford Bookstore on the Stanford Campus or at the first couple rehearsals. The total cost for music this quarter (Spring, 2002) is $18.00 (rounded up $0.19 to avoid hassles with change).

To speed things up, you might want to print out this registration page or this pdf version and fill it out before you come to the first rehearsal.

Current Progam

This quarter's (Spring, 2002) choral music is the Bruckner Te Deum and Brahms Gesang der Parzen.

Rehearsals

Rehearsals are held Monday evenings from 7:15 to 9:45 in Campbell Recital Hall in the Braun Music Building, but please be early for the first one, since it will take extra time to get everyone signed up and to distribute the music, which will be available at the rehearsal as well as at the Stanford Bookstore. Sign ups start at 6:45 PM.

In addition there are usually some optional sectional rehearsals on Wednesday afternoons. Just before the performance we typically have two "dress rehearsals", also mandatory, when we rehearse with the orchestra, usually in the hall in which we will be performing. Concert dress is not required or even recommended at these rehearsals. Other rehearsals are typically accompanied by piano only.

Members may miss up to two mandatory rehearsals. Members who miss more than two mandatory rehearsals without prior arrangement with the director will not be able to participate in the performance.

Performances

There are two performances this quarter on March 1 and March 2, 2002, in Memorial Church on the Stanford campus.

Concert Dress

Men wear tuxedos or a tuxedo-like suits (i.e., black; not grey, brown or blue), black shoes and socks. Women are all in black: long dress, or long black skirt or slacks with long-sleeved (preferably) or short-sleeved black blouse, and black shoes. Please no sleeveless tops and no flashy jewelry.

Concert Aromas

Several people in the chorus are particularly sensitive to scented products such as perfume and hair spray; all of us have to sing in close quarters and benefit by having air to breathe which is as pure as possible. On all rehearsal days, and especially on dress rehearsal and performance days, we request you do not use any such products. For information about scent-free alternatives, please contact Courtney Behm at Courtney@ViewpointSolutions.com.

Covering Your Music

For performances music must be put in a black folder or notebook, or bound with a black cover. Don't wait until the last minute to find a cover which will be practical in performance!

Expectations

Each singer is expected to learn the music assigned for each rehearsal on his or her own. Beyond the first reading of a work, rehearsal time will be devoted to the development of ensemble sound, interpretation, and other musical matters. It is further expected that each singer will make a reasonable effort to develop his or her own vocal skills, and will make every effort to contribute in a positive manner to the work of the group.

Tickets

Tickets are usually offered for sale at rehearsals starting roughly a couple weeks before performancs. They are also available at the Stanford box office in Tresidder ((650) 725-ARTS) and at the door.
