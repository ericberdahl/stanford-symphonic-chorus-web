Joining the Chorus

The Stanford Chorus is open to all students, faculty, staff and any other residents of the community interested in singing wonderful music. New members will be auditioned prior to the first rehearsal of each quarter. The purpose of the audition is not to weed people out; rather it gives our director a chance to get to know the voices which will comprise the Chorus.

Members who are not students or faculty pay a modest (currently $25) participation fee. This money helps to offset expenses, the largest of which is hiring orchestras.

Music

The music to be performed can be purchased at the Stanford Bookstore on the Stanford Campus or (usually) at the first couple rehearsals. This quarter's concert (Winter, 1996) will be an all-Beethoven affair. It will open with the Egmont Overture. We will then perform Meeresstille und glückliche Fahrt and the Mass in C, joined by soloists Kristin Genis, soprano, Claire Giovannetti, mezzo-soprano, Greg Wait, tenor, and Perry Cook, baritone. Rehearsals

Rehearsals are generaly held Monday evenings from 7:15 to 9:45 in Campbell Recital Hall. In addition there are usually some (optional) sectional rehearsals on Wednesday afternoons. Just before the performance we have two "dress rehearsals" (concert dress is not required or even recommended at these rehearsals), also mandatory, when we rehearse with the orchestra, usually in the hall in which we will be performing. Other rehearsals are accompanied by piano only.

Members may miss up to three mandatory rehearsals. Members who miss more mandatory rehearsals without prior arrangement with the director will not be able to participate in the performance.

Performances

There are usually two performances of the quarter's program. This winter the performances will be March 1st and 2nd in Memorial Church.

Concert Dress:Men wear tuxedos or a tuxedo-like suits (i.e., black; not grey, brown or blue), black shoes and socks. Women are all in black: long black skirt or slacks, long-sleeved black blouse, and black shoes. Flashy jewelry, perfume and cologne are all to be avoided. Music should be put in a black folder or notebook of some sort.
