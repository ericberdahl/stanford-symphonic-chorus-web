Welcome

The Chorus

The Stanford Symphonic Chorus is a group of approximately 180 students, faculty, staff, and members of the community led by music director Stephen Sano. The Chorus generally performs three works a year, one each academic quarter. Each performance typically features a large choral work, and includes other choral or instrumental pieces. See our Performances page for information about past performances.

Hector Berlioz, Requiem

Hector Berlioz's Grande Messe des morts, Op. 5, one of his best-known works, is scored for a large orchestration of woodwind and brass instruments, including four antiphonal brass ensembles, and for chorus placed thoughout the hall. Come see how we do it.
